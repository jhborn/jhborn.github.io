Bettles

Head

The head, having mouthparts projecting forward or sometimes downturned, is usually heavily sclerotized and is sometimes very large

mouthparts

Beetles have mouthparts like those of grasshoppers.

Thorax

two discernible parts,

legs

The multisegmented legs end in two to five small segments called tarsi.

prothorax and peterothorax The pterothorax is the fused meso- and metathorax, which are commonly separated in other insect species

wingsThe forewings of beetles are not used for flight, but form elytra which cover the hind part of the body and protect the hindwings. The elytra are usually hard shell-like structures which must be raised to allow the hindwings to move for flight.

abdomenThe abdomen is the section behind the metathorax, made up of a series of rings, each with a hole for breathing and respiration, called a spiracle,
